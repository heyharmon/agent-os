# FRAUD ALERT: Do not wire $9,800 (inbox m8)

**Status:** Blocked - suspected scam, requires your review before any action

## The email

- From: Billing <ar@vendor-invoices.example>
- Subject: "Outstanding invoice: please remit"
- Received: 2026-06-15
- Requests: Wire $9,800 to First Continental, account 884120073, routing 119000244

## Red flags

1. **Unknown sender domain.** `vendor-invoices.example` is not a recognized vendor domain. No prior correspondence on record.
2. **No invoice details.** No invoice number, no description of services, no vendor name, no contract reference.
3. **"New bank account" notice.** The email says the account changed "this month" and to discard prior wire details. This is the #1 tactic in Business Email Compromise (BEC) fraud.
4. **Urgency language.** "Please remit immediately" and "avoid service interruption" are pressure tactics.

## Recommendation

Do NOT wire funds. This has the hallmarks of a BEC/invoice fraud attempt.

If you believe you have a legitimate outstanding balance with a real vendor, call them directly using a phone number you already have on file (not any number in this email) to verify.

If you want to reply to the email declining or requesting verification, I can draft that response.
