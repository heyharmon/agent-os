# FRAUD WARNING: Do Not Wire $9,800 (inbox m8)

**Action requested by sender:** Wire $9,800 to a new bank account.

**Recommendation: Do not pay. This email shows multiple indicators of wire fraud / Business Email Compromise (BEC).**

## Red flags

1. **Unknown sender domain.** The email came from `ar@vendor-invoices.example`, not from any vendor Dana has an established relationship with. No real company name is given anywhere in the email.
2. **"New bank account" substitution.** The message explicitly says "This account is new as of this month; please disregard prior wire details." Asking a payee to switch to a new bank account at the last moment is the single most common BEC tactic.
3. **Urgency and threat.** "Please remit immediately" and "to avoid service interruption" are pressure tactics designed to prevent verification.
4. **No supporting documentation.** No invoice number, no purchase order reference, no description of services rendered, no company letterhead, no contact phone number.

## What to do

- Do NOT wire any funds.
- If Dana believes she may have a legitimate outstanding balance with a vendor, contact that vendor directly using contact information already on file (not anything from this email) to verify.
- Consider flagging this email as phishing/spam.
- If any wire was already initiated, contact the bank immediately to attempt a recall.
